# Public Timing Batch Example

## Status

Public-safe demo example.

This file shows how captured signals may be displayed after a Pain Timing Map review.

This is not:

- a build recommendation
- a priority decision
- a public intake result
- a registry promotion
- a sell-test plan
- a release decision

## Purpose

Pain Timing Map does not turn every captured signal into a task.

It first places signals on different lines:

- Timing Line: when the pain may become visible or stronger
- Deadline Line: when delay creates irreversible loss
- Trigger Line: when a specific event should activate the check

## Demo Captures

### DEMO-001 - AI Paper Delta Score

A way to evaluate AI-generated research notes by structural delta, not by polish.

### DEMO-002 - EV-Based Fix Filter

A way to separate high-value fixes from low-value cleanup after an AI-assisted audit.

### DEMO-003 - Fable Audit

A check used before high-stakes public, pricing, contract, or irreversible GO decisions.

### DEMO-004 - Patent Deadline Card

A deadline-tracking card for irreversible rights or filing windows.

### DEMO-005 - Small Curiosity Signal

A low-stakes signal that is not worth building yet, but should not be discarded.

### DEMO-006 - Small Everyday Memory

A relational AI memory signal about small personally meaningful fragments.

```text
Before public / contract / pricing / high-risk GO decisions
|- DEMO-003 Fable Audit

When repair candidates multiply after an AI-assisted audit
|- DEMO-002 EV-Based Fix Filter

When AI-generated research or paper comparison increases
|- DEMO-001 AI Paper Delta Score

When small, low-seriousness curiosity signals recur
|- DEMO-005 Small Curiosity Signal

When relational AI fails to reconnect small everyday fragments
|- DEMO-006 Small Everyday Memory
```

## What This Shows

Captured signals are not immediately promoted into projects.

They are first placed on a map.

This helps separate:

- what is becoming painful soon,
- what depends on a trigger,
- what has a deadline,
- what should be parked,
- what needs more evidence.
